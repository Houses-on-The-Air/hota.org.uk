About Houses on The Air (HOTA)

Houses on The Air (HOTA) is an amateur radio activity that encourages operators to set up and operate from various houses. Inspired by the popular Parks on The Air (POTA) program, HOTA aims to promote the hobby of amateur radio and provide a fun and engaging way for operators to explore new locations.

How to Call CQ for HOTA

Calling CQ is a way to initiate contact with other amateur radio operators. Here are the steps to call CQ with HOTA:

  1. Find a clear frequency on the band you wish to operate.
  2. Listen for a few moments to ensure the frequency is not in use.
  3. Announce your call sign and your intention to call CQ, for example: "Is this frequency in use? This is [Your Call Sign]."
  4. If the frequency is clear, call CQ: "CQ CQ CQ, this is [Your Call Sign] calling CQ for Houses on The Air. CQ CQ CQ, this is [Your Call Sign] calling CQ and standing by."
  5. Listen for a response. If you hear a reply, acknowledge it and proceed with the contact.
  6. Exchange signal reports, names, and locations with the other operator.
  7. Log the contact details for your records.

For FT8 operation, follow these steps:

  1. Set up your FT8 software and ensure it is configured correctly with your call sign and grid locator.
  2. Tune to the FT8 frequency for the band you wish to operate.
  3. Monitor the waterfall display for a clear spot to transmit.
  4. Initiate a CQ call by selecting an empty spot and clicking the "CQ" button in your software. Your CQ message will be automatically transmitted.
  5. Wait for a response. If you receive a reply, your software will decode the message and display the call sign of the responding station.
  6. Follow the on-screen prompts to complete the contact, exchanging signal reports and other information as required.
  7. Log the contact details for your records.

Remember to be courteous and follow all amateur radio regulations while operating.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about HOTA

What is Houses on The Air (HOTA)?

HOTA is an amateur radio activity that encourages operators to set up and operate from various houses, promoting the hobby of amateur radio.

Does it have to be a house?

Yes. In order to maintain the unique value of HOTA, we are only allowing houses.

How do I participate in HOTA?

You can participate by setting up your amateur radio station at different houses and making contacts with other operators. Check our activities section for more details.

Do I need a special license to participate?

No special license is required, but you must have a valid amateur radio license to operate.

Can I earn awards by participating in HOTA?

Yes, we offer various awards for different levels of participation and achievements. Check our contests and awards section for more information.

How can I contact HOTA for more information?

You can reach us at info@hota.org.uk.
